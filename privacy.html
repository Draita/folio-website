Privacy policy

Last updated: 9 July 2026

Folio is a local-first audiobook library application developed by Draita. This policy explains what Folio accesses, what stays on your device, and what happens when you enable optional online features.

What Folio accesses

Folio can access audiobook folders and app-owned files that you choose. On Android, folder scanning may require storage access. This access is used to find audio files, read supported metadata, create library indexes, save playback progress, and display covers. Folio does not sell your library or use it for advertising.

Data that stays on your device

Your local audio files, folder paths, library database, playback position, bookmarks, settings, scan fingerprints, and generated metadata remain on your device unless you explicitly enable a feature that sends selected data elsewhere. App-owned cached files can be removed from Folio settings or by uninstalling the app.

Optional online features

  • Metadata and cover lookup: searches and requests you initiate may be sent to the selected metadata provider. Provider servers may receive normal network information such as your IP address.
  • Google Drive sync: if enabled, Folio uses your Google account authorization and sends the library metadata or files you choose to sync to Google Drive. Google’s policies apply to Google’s processing.
  • Public-domain catalogues: when you browse or download LibriVox content, Folio communicates with LibriVox and Archive.org. Downloaded files are saved where you choose.
  • Torrent import: if you use the torrent feature, your device communicates with trackers and peers required by the torrent. Use only content you are authorized to access.

Sharing and sale

Draita does not sell personal information. Folio does not share your local library with Draita. Optional third-party services receive only the requests, account data, metadata, or files needed for the feature you activate.

Security, retention, and deletion

OAuth credentials are stored using the platform secure-storage facility where supported. Data stored by Folio remains until you remove it, clear the app, uninstall Folio, disconnect an optional service, or request help. To disconnect Google Drive, use Folio’s Drive settings and revoke access from your Google account. To request deletion of data held by Draita, contact the developer using the public GitHub issue tracker linked below; include no private files or credentials.

Children

Folio is not directed to children under 13. Do not use Folio to upload or share a child’s personal information through optional online services.

Contact

Privacy contact and support: github.com/Draita/Folio/issues. Developer: Draita. This policy applies to Folio and may be updated when the app’s data practices change.
